<p>What if we&apos;re all here for a reason?  What if we all have this amazing potential to create our own reality?  What if life is more than a collection of memories, but a choice?  I&apos;m always soul searching.  Looking for the answers.  Yesterday I stumbled on Oprah&apos;s show, and I haven&apos;t watched it in years but I thought I should try to start since it&apos;s her last season.  I watched, listened, absorbed and turned the TV off, but it stuck  like a good book or movie that you ponder for hours afterwards.  Without telling you everything, I&apos;ll just say it&apos;s about miracles.  Do you believe?  Maybe not, and maybe that&apos;s why you&apos;re stuck.  I think achieving greatness is a bit of a miracle.  You have to believe.  I struggle with this daily, but remedy the struggles by reminding myself that I am worthy of greatness, of happiness, of complete joy.  I have attached the bits that stuck with me, but you can read the whole article <a href="http://http://www.oprah.com/spirit/Do-You-Believe-in-Miracles_1/1">here</a>.</p>
<p>&#8220;I believe the power of belief, the power of the mind, are far more powerful than we have begun to explore,&#8221; he says. &#8220;I believe that&apos;s an unexplored wilderness in terms of research.&#8221;</p>
<p>In terms of his own experience with John of God, Dr. Rediger says it&apos;s been life-changing. &#8220;Perhaps the real heart within us is not just a pump, he says. &#8220;Perhaps the real heart within us is about love and faith. Perhaps the physical body is not who we really are. Perhaps we are these invisible souls walking around, and the body is just an instrument or metaphor for something we are trying to learn.&#8221;</p>
